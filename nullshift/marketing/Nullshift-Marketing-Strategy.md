# Nullshift Development Ltd — Master Marketing Strategy

*Prepared June 2026. North-star goal: **lock in recurring revenue (MRR)**. Budget posture: modest paid (£300–£1,000/mo) on an organic + founder-led core. This document is the single source of truth for the Marketing Advisor agent, the strategy document, and the dashboard.*

---

## 0. The one-paragraph thesis

Nullshift is currently sold as a **bespoke build shop** ("we make you a website, you own it") with a learning subscription bolted on the side. That is a good product trapped in a one-off-revenue business model. The highest-leverage move — and the one that serves the recurring-revenue goal — is to **reposition Nullshift from "we build websites" to "we run the system that brings you customers," sold as a monthly subscription with a small setup fee.** We do this by going *narrow first* — owning UK **trades/home-services** with a single productized offer ("Never Miss a Job"), then cloning the exact playbook into **health & wellness** ("Zero No-Show") and later **accountants** (riding the Making Tax Digital deadline). The unfair advantages we already own and barely use — the **Systems Lab live demos**, the **"own it, no monthly ransom" ethic**, **2–4 week delivery**, and **fixed pricing** — become the spearpoint. The result is predictable MRR with ~85% gross margins, an ROI story so concrete it closes itself, and a moat no template-shop or faceless automation agency can copy.

## 3. The strategic bet — who we sell to first (and why)

The recurring-revenue thesis needs five things to line up at once: **(1) huge volume, (2) recurring, quantifiable pain, (3) ability to pay monthly, (4) low competition for "bespoke + automation + retainer," (5) easily repeatable templates.** Only two verticals score high on all five. We sequence them.

### #1 — TRADES / HOME SERVICES *(the wedge to dominate first)*
Plumbers, electricians, heating engineers, builders, roofers, landscapers.

- **Volume:** construction is the **#1 UK business sector (15.8% of all businesses)** — ~97,000 contractor businesses, ~748,000 self-employed workers, ~59% sole traders/partnerships. This is the exact owner-operator buyer we want.
- **The killer pain (this *is* the pitch):** UK trades lose **~£24,000/year to missed calls**; ~60–70% of inbound calls go unanswered (they're up a ladder) and **85% of callers won't leave a voicemail** — they ring the next Google result within 30 minutes.
- **Ability to pay:** electrician/plumber day rates average **~£330**. One recovered job per month more than covers a £149–£399/mo plan. The ROI math closes the sale for us.
- **Low competition:** existing trade-site agencies (TradePal, Steony, Simple Trade Websites) mostly sell *static lead-gen sites* — not integrated automation + a care plan. The recurring-revenue lane is wide open.
- **Templatable:** every trade site has the same skeleton (services, service areas, reviews, quote form) → fast, repeatable, high-margin delivery.

**The offer that prints MRR:** *"Never Miss a Job"* — a bespoke fast site **plus** missed-call-text-back, a 24/7 AI receptionist/chat, instant quote-form follow-up, automated review generation, and Google Business Profile + local SEO, wrapped in a monthly plan. Sell the **outcome (jobs recovered)**, not "website maintenance."

### #2 — HEALTH & WELLNESS *(clone the playbook)*
Independent salons, barbers, nail/aesthetics, physios, dental, gyms, PTs.

- **Volume:** ~51,800 hair & beauty businesses, **60.5% self-employed**, ~80% with <5 staff; plus ~12,000 dental practices, ~5,200 gyms, ~24,900 PTs.
- **The pain:** no-shows cost UK salons **~£1.6bn/year (~£2m/day)**, ~£39 each, up to **20% of revenue**. Deposits + reminders cut no-shows **70–85%**.
- **Demand is structural:** **70%+ of consumers prefer to book online**, **40% of bookings happen after hours**, and 24/7 booking lifts bookings ~26–37%.

**The offer:** *"Zero No-Show"* — bespoke booking site **plus** online booking with deposits, automated SMS/email reminders, waitlist auto-fill, rebooking nudges, and post-visit review requests. Headline: *"Cut no-shows by up to 85% and fill your calendar while you sleep."*

### #3 — ACCOUNTANTS / BOOKKEEPERS *(the tailwind play, later)*
**Making Tax Digital for Income Tax is mandatory from April 2026** (sole traders/landlords >£50k), extending to >£30k (2027) and >£20k (2028). That's a forced wave of "client portal + automated document collection + onboarding" demand. Trust-heavy, slower sale — run it as a targeted Q3/Q4 campaign, not the opening move.

> **Deprioritise for now:** restaurants (thin margins, declining count, crowded tooling), general retail/e-commerce (most competitive, least automation-shaped), broad professional services (slow, saturated).

## 4. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Primary — "Gary the Sole-Trader Sparky."** UK owner-operated trade (plumber/electrician/builder/heating), 1–10 staff, sole trader or small Ltd, ~£300+ day rate, runs the business from his phone and van. Has no website or a neglected one, relies on Checkatrade + word of mouth, and loses 2–3 jobs a week to missed calls without realising the annual scale (£24k). **Buying trigger:** lost a big job to a missed call, a competitor outranks him on Google, or he's drowning in admin.

**Secondary — "Sophie the Salon Owner."** Independent salon/barber/clinic owner, 1–5 staff/chairs, takes bookings by phone/DM/paper, bleeds 5–20% of revenue to no-shows and after-hours missed bookings. **Buying trigger:** a string of no-shows, a fully-booked competitor, maternity cover, or scaling to a second chair/location.

Both share the same psychology: **time-poor, results-driven, allergic to jargon, sceptical of "tech people," and motivated by money recovered, not features.** Sell them outcomes in pounds.

## 5. The recurring-revenue engine — offer architecture

**The repositioning principle:** stop selling *deliverables*, start selling *outcomes as subscriptions*. The build is the on-ramp; the monthly plan is the business.

### Resolving the "own it vs subscription" tension (critical)
Nullshift's brand is "own your code, no monthly ransom." A monthly model could look hypocritical — so we make the distinction explicit and turn it into the killer differentiator:

> **"Own your system. Subscribe to results."**
> You own the code. Every account (hosting, domain, booking, AI) is in *your* name. Cancel any time and keep everything. The monthly fee isn't rent on your website — it's us **running the machine that brings you customers**: managing the automations, optimising your leads/bookings, adding new ones, reporting on the money recovered, and being on call. No hostage situation. No rebilled-tool markups. This is the one thing WaaS shops and automation agencies *cannot* say.

### The productized ladder (this is what drives MRR)

| Plan | Setup | Monthly | What's inside |
|---|---|---|---|
| Foundation | £499 | **£149/mo** | Bespoke fast site, missed-call-text-back, automated review engine, Google Business Profile optimisation, monthly "jobs recovered" report |

| Pro | £1,199 | **£399/mo** | + full CRM, quote→invoice automation, multi-channel automations, priority support & a quarterly strategy call |

**HEALTH & WELLNESS — "Zero No-Show"**

|---|---|---|---|

**Keep & reframe the existing tiers:**
- **Partner £749/mo** → premium "**done-with-you**: we build it and teach you to own it in 12 months." Perfect for ambitious clients who want independence *and* hand-holding. High-value, sticky.
- **Maintenance £249/mo** → folded into the care plans above (don't sell it naked).
- **Learning subs (Core £19.99 / Grow £49 / Pro £249)** → repositioned as the **self-serve entry + ascension + nurture** layer: a low-risk "start here" for DIY-ers, a tripwire after lead magnets, and a place to park leads who aren't ready for done-for-you. Many will ascend to a Growth System plan.

**Why this wins on margin:** the automation layer (missed-call text-back, reminders, review requests, AI chat) costs little to run and ~nothing to replicate per client → **~85% gross margin on monthly**, and delivery gets cheaper every time because trade/wellness sites are templatable.

- *"Never miss another job."* (trades)
- *"Own your system. Subscribe to results."* (brand-level recurring)

**The unfair demo move:** put the **Systems Lab** in front of every prospect. "Don't take my word for it — here's the actual booking system / AI receptionist, click it." Nobody else lets buyers test-drive. Turn each demo into a 15–30s vertical video for ads and outreach.

## 7. Channel strategy (organic core + £300–£1,000/mo paid)

Ranked by ROI for a local-SMB, recurring-revenue motion:

**Tier 1 — do these first, they convert.**
1. **Google Search Ads (high-intent, local).** Point the **majority of the paid budget** here. Bid on "electrician website," "[trade] website [town]," "salon booking system," "[trade] near me" (for retarget). Small budget works because intent is red-hot and geography is tight. Target **£300–£700/mo** to start.
2. **Founder-led outbound (near-zero cost, highest close rate).** 15–25 personalised teardowns/week: a 60-second Loom showing the prospect's current site/Google listing problem + the fix + a Systems Lab link. This is the single best channel for the first 10–20 clients.
3. **Local SEO + Google Business Profile.** Niche landing pages at scale (trade × town, e.g. /electrician-website-leeds). Own "money keyword" local searches organically over 60–90 days.

**Tier 2 — compounding, build in parallel.**
4. **Short-form video (TikTok/Insta Reels/YouTube Shorts/LinkedIn).** Site teardowns, "the £24k phone call," Systems Lab clips, before/afters. Cheap, demonstrative, algorithm-friendly.
5. **Meta/Instagram ads (retargeting + lookalikes).** Especially for wellness (visual). Spend **£150–£300/mo** retargeting site/demo visitors and calculator users. Demo-led creative.
6. **Lead magnets / calculators.** "Missed-Call Revenue Calculator" (trades) and "No-Show Cost Calculator" (wellness) — interactive tools that quantify the prospect's pain in pounds, then route to a call or the £19.99 learning tier. High-converting, shareable, SEO-friendly.

**Tier 3 — multiplier channels.**
7. **Niche partnerships & referrals.** Trade suppliers/merchants, salon wholesalers, trade associations, local business networks (BNI/chambers), accountants who serve trades. Build a **referral program** (e.g. one free month or £100 per referred client that signs).
8. **Case studies & social proof engine.** Every client becomes a "we recovered £X/£ filled Y appointments" story. Make collecting a video testimonial part of onboarding.
9. **Email nurture.** Calculators + learning subs feed a list; monthly "money you're leaving on the table" emails convert over time.

**Paid budget starting allocation (£600/mo example):** Google Search £400 · Meta retargeting £150 · creative/tools/boosts £50. Scale Google first when CAC proves out.

## 11. Unit economics & the MRR model

**Per-client (blended Foundation/Growth assumptions):**
- Setup fee: **~£600** (covers most/all of acquisition + build cost up front → fast payback).
- Monthly: **~£220** at **~85% gross margin** → ~£187 gross profit/mo.
- Expected retention: **18–24 months** (sticky because it's tied to their lead flow/bookings).
- **LTV ≈ £600 + (£187 × ~21) ≈ £4,500.**
- **Target CAC < £400 blended** → **LTV:CAC > 10:1** in the ideal case; even at £800 all-in CAC, the setup fee makes payback near-immediate.

**Scaling the MRR (illustrative):**
- 10 clients × £220 = **£2,200 MRR (~£26k ARR)**
- 25 clients × £240 (mix-shift up) = **£6,000 MRR (~£72k ARR)**
- 50 clients × £260 = **£13,000 MRR (~£156k ARR)**

The flywheel: every build funds the next month's ads; every client adds compounding MRR + a case study + referrals; every niche template makes delivery cheaper. That is how you scale "at an unprecedented scale" *without* unprecedented spend.

## 13. Risks & watch-outs

- **Don't compete on cheapest monthly** vs WaaS — you'll lose and erode margin. Win on outcome + ownership.
- **Delivery bottleneck:** as a founder-led shop, MRR growth can outrun delivery. Protect margin with niche templates and a productised scope; raise prices before you drown.
- **Churn risk if value isn't visible:** the monthly report ("jobs recovered / no-shows prevented") is non-negotiable — it's what justifies the subscription every month.
- **Brand consistency:** keep the "own it / no ransom" ethic loud even while selling recurring — it's the differentiator, not a contradiction.
- **Niche focus discipline:** resist going horizontal again. Dominate trades, *then* clone. One playbook, repeated, beats five half-built ones.
