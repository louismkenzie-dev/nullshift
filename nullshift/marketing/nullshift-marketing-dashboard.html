The strategic bet — who we sell to first

Recurring revenue needs five things at once: huge volume, recurring & quantifiable pain, ability to pay monthly, low competition, and repeatable templates. Two verticals score high on all five. We dominate one, then clone the playbook.

#1 · Dominate first

Trades / Home Services

Plumbers, electricians, builders, heating engineers. The UK's #1 business sector (15.8%). They lose ~£24k/yr to missed calls and 85% of callers never leave a voicemail.

  • Offer: "Never Miss a Job" £149–£399/mo
  • One recovered job (£330) covers the plan
  • Incumbents sell static sites — retainer lane is open
#2 · Clone the playbook

Health & Wellness

Salons, barbers, clinics, physios, gyms. ~51,800 hair & beauty firms, 60% self-employed. No-shows eat up to 20% of revenue; deposits cut them 70–85%.

  • Offer: "Zero No-Show" £129–£349/mo
  • 70% prefer to book online; 40% after hours
  • Visual niche — perfect for demo-led ads
#3 · Tailwind, later

Accountants (MTD)

Making Tax Digital is mandatory from April 2026 (>£50k), then £30k, then £20k. A forced wave of "client portal + document automation" demand.

  • Offer: client portal + onboarding automation
  • Trust-heavy, slower sale → Q3/Q4 campaign
  • Run after the trades engine is humming

Deprioritised for now: restaurants (thin margins, declining count), retail/e-commerce (most competitive), broad professional services (slow, saturated). Focus beats spread.

Channel strategy — organic core + £300–£1,000/mo paid

Ranked by ROI for a local-SMB recurring motion. Put the paid budget where intent is hottest (Google Search), let founder-led outbound win the first clients, and build compounding organic in parallel.

Tier 1 — do first, they convert

Most of paid budget

Google Search Ads

High-intent, local: "[trade] website [town]", "salon booking system". £300–£700/mo — small budget works because intent is red-hot and geography is tight.

Near-zero cost · best close rate

Founder-led outbound

15–25 personalised 60-sec Loom teardowns/week showing their listing problem + the fix + a Systems Lab link. Best channel for your first 10–20 clients.

Compounds 60–90 days

Local SEO + GBP

Niche pages at scale (trade × town). Own the "money keyword" local searches organically.

Tier 2 — build in parallel

Short-form video

Site teardowns, "the £24k phone call", Systems Lab clips. Cheap, demonstrative, algorithm-friendly.

Meta retargeting

£150–£300/mo retargeting demo/calculator visitors. Especially strong for wellness (visual).

Lead-magnet calculators

"Missed-Call Revenue" & "No-Show Cost" tools quantify pain in £, then route to a call (see Numbers tab).

Tier 3 — multipliers

Partnerships & referrals

Trade merchants, salon wholesalers, associations, chambers. Referral reward: a free month or £100 per signed client.

Case-study engine

Every client = "we recovered £X / filled Y appts". Make a video testimonial part of onboarding.

Email nurture

Calculators + subs feed a list; monthly "money you're leaving on the table" emails convert over time.

Signature campaigns

Flagship

"The £24,000 Phone Call"

Calculator → landing page → ad → outreach, all on the missed-call stat. "How many calls did you miss this week?" Drives Never-Miss-a-Job.

Wellness

"Your Empty Chair"

No-show cost calculator. "Every empty chair this week cost you £X — here's how to fill it automatically."

Brand / values

Anti-ransom vs Wix & lock-in agencies. "Stop renting your website. Own your system." Great organic + PR.

Demo-led

"Try Before You Build"

Push the live Systems Lab. "Test-drive your future booking system right now." Repurpose into ad creative.
